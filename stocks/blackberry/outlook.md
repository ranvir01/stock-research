## 1. QNX ↔ BlackBerry: how the "child company" pays you

- QNX is a **wholly-owned, fully consolidated division** — BlackBerry bought QNX Software Systems outright from Harman in 2010 (~$200M). There is no minority interest, no separate stock, no royalty pass-through. **Every dollar of QNX revenue and its ~80%+ gross margin lands directly in BB's consolidated income statement and EPS.** Owning BB *is* owning QNX — no leakage.
- History worth knowing: in 2023 BlackBerry publicly explored **spinning off / IPO-ing the IoT unit (QNX)**, then shelved it in early 2024. No active process exists today, but a sum-of-the-parts unlock (QNX alone could command richer auto-software multiples than consolidated BB) remains a latent catalyst if management ever revisits it.

## 5. Industry — is QNX's position getting stronger or weaker?

**Incrementally stronger.** QNX owns the safety-critical microkernel/hypervisor layer (~36–38% of automotive OS share, concentrated in safety) where ASIL-D recertification costs make switching a multi-year, multi-million-dollar ordeal. Android Automotive competes at the *infotainment* layer — the standard architecture is QNX-for-safety + Android-for-cockpit, complementary not zero-sum. Even OEMs building their own stacks (Mercedes MB.OS) reportedly keep QNX at the safety core.

Ranked threats by 2028–2030 (agent probabilities): OEM vertical integration squeezing per-vehicle royalties ~35%; NVIDIA/Qualcomm reference stacks commoditizing QNX into a substitutable line-item (DriveOS supports QNX **and** Linux — QNX is a partner, not exclusive) ~30%; Android scope-creep ~25%. Robotics/"physical AI": real but early — an estimated **incremental $30–60M by FY29**, optionality rather than a second growth engine yet. Secure Comms is **growth, not a melting ice cube** (NATO clearance, Five Eyes trust, rising defense budgets). The one event that would flip the industry verdict: a major OEM publicly dropping QNX from the *safety layer* of a next-gen platform.

## 6. Synthesis — where BB is heading

- **Company: up and to the right.** Real revenue/EBITDA growth with visibility (backlog + sovereign contracts), strengthening moat, credible optionality. This is no longer a meme shell; it's a Rule-of-40 embedded-software company.
- **Stock, next 2 months: most likely digestion, not explosion.** The probability-weighted agent view puts ~70–75% odds on the stock NOT being meaningfully higher in 8 weeks (chop or pullback), because the fuel is retail flow, the smart-money trail points the other way, and the valuation already pays for FY29.
- **Stock, 12 months: $12–15 base case** — roughly flat from $13, with fat tails both ways ($6–8 bear, $17–20 bull).
- **For a new investor at $13:** small starter position or wait for the $9–11 pullback zone; don't buy a full position into RSI-88 extension. **The dip-buy zone if momentum cracks is $8–9.**
