# BlackBerry (BB) — Investment thesis

Produced July 1, 2026 via a 3-agent adversarial debate (Bull vs Bear, 2 rounds with rebuttals, arbitrated on Claude Fable 5). Price reference: ~$13.07 (52-wk range $3.12–$13.59).

## One-sentence thesis

> BlackBerry is a genuine QNX-led turnaround whose stock has already priced in years of flawless execution — worth owning only in small size with strict exits, not at a 5,000-share concentration.

## Business I think I'm buying

- **QNX:** Embedded safety-critical OS. Record quarterly revenue (~$72–79M), ~$950M royalty backlog, 275M+ vehicles, Rule of 40, FY27 guide $295–312M. New "physical AI" vector via April 2026 NVIDIA IGX Thor integration; robotics/industrial already ~20% of segment revenue. Design wins: BMW, Volvo, Mercedes, Leapmotor.
- **Secure Communications:** Government/defense UEM & sovereign comms (Cylance was divested to Arctic Wolf in Feb 2025 — BB is no longer a consumer-endpoint player). Canada SecuSUITE contract renewed/expanded March 2026 through **2033**; DND phased deployment starts late 2026. Rule of 40 in Q1.
- **Other / legacy:** Licensing residuals; immaterial to thesis. **Not a phone company** — 2026 keyboard phones (Clicks, Unihertz) are third-party, $0 to BB.

## Bull case (from the Bull agent, post-rebuttal)

**What must go right:**
1. Beat-and-raise cadence continues (Q2 FY27 ~Sept 24, 2026); QNX backlog converts to royalties on schedule.
2. NVIDIA/robotics "physical AI" wins become disclosed revenue, not just narrative.
3. Growth investors re-rate a Rule-of-40 software asset; multiple stays elevated while EBITDA compounds (+144% YoY in Q1).

**Rough 12–24 mo upside target:** $18–20 (backlog conversion + re-rating)

**Probability estimate:** ~25%

## Base case

**Most likely path:**
- Guidance ($594–621M rev, $119–139M adj EBITDA) roughly met; multiple normalizes as growth "won't be linear" (management's own words); stock chops between the breakout zone and the highs.

**Fair value range:** $10 – $14 (Bull said $13–15 @ 50%; Bear said $9–10 @ 40%; arbiter blends to $10–14)

**Probability estimate:** ~45–50%

## Bear case (from the Bear agent, post-rebuttal)

**What breaks:**
1. Multiple compression: ~55–60x EV/EBITDA on FY27 guidance is CrowdStrike/Palantir-tier pricing; most analyst targets ($7.58–$13) sit at/below spot.
2. Third euphoric spike unwinds like Jan/Jun 2021 (−60–70% both times); May–Jun 2026 retail volume broke Jan-2021 records; RSI 79–88 with price 29%/155% above 20/200-day SMAs.
3. QNX moat is the safety microkernel, not the whole car — OEM in-house stacks (Cariad, MB.OS, GM, Rivian) cap the TAM story; royalty conversion is slow and auto-cyclical.

**Downside target:** $5–6 (2021-pattern round trip); first support $9.84 (20-day SMA), then $8–9 breakout zone

**Probability estimate:** ~25–30%

## Moat & edge

- Do I have an information edge? **No.** Insider Form 4s show zero open-market buys in 2026 — no "they know something" signal; CEO/CFO sales were RSU/tax mechanics at $3.56–$3.88. The rally is fully explained by public catalysts (Q1 beat, raised guidance, NVIDIA deal, upgrades, retail flow).
- Why might the market be wrong? Either direction: analysts anchored to pre-inflection models may under-rate backlog conversion (bull), or retail flow has overshot fundamentals as it did twice in 2021 (bear). Probability-weighted EVs from the debate: Bull ~$14 (+7%), Bear ~$11 (−16%) — thin-to-negative expected return with meme-stock variance.
